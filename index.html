✅ What Is QTAM?

The Quick Technical Architecture Method (QTAM) is a streamlined approach to architecture work—perfect for technical professionals who need to move fast, stay clear, and align with stakeholders.

Inspired by the TOGAF® ADM but redesigned for real-world agility, QTAM breaks down architectural analysis into 6 focused actions.

🎯 Design the Architecture Vision
Define the purpose, scope, and stakeholders. Establish the "why" behind the architecture effort and align expectations early.
✅ Clarifies goals and aligns expectations upfront.

🏢 Design the Business Architecture
Map the business context. Understand the value chain, impacted roles, and how architecture influences business operations.
✅ Captures the business view to ensure relevance and alignment.

🧩 Design the Information Systems Architecture
Identify the key applications and data flows. Ensure information systems support the targeted business change.
✅ Connects the business vision to system behaviors and data flows.

🔌 Design the Technology Architecture
Define the infrastructure and technologies. Explore implementation options and assess feasibility.
✅ Aligns technical choices with operational and project constraints.

📦 Consolidate the Work Packages
Translate analysis into tangible work. Group gaps and tasks into actionable packages with effort and dependencies.
✅ Turns insights into plans your teams can act on.

🧭 Design the Course of Action
Build the transformation roadmap. Sequence work packages, define milestones, and identify risks.
✅ Provides stakeholders with a clear, value-driven implementation plan.
